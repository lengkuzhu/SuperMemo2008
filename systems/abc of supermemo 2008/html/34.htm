Subject: Topic #34: Incremental reading

Even though you have already learned the basics of SuperMemo, you should know that the program provides you with a yet more powerful learning weapon: Incremental reading.

Incremental reading is more complex than basic SuperMemo, and may require more training. But it will help you read thousands of articles imported from the Internet. It will also help you quickly convert these articles into rock-solid knowlege.

Press Enter for some incremental reading hints.

You can read more about incremental reading at supermemo.com: Incremental reading

Topic #43: Importing articles (8 elements)

Incremental reading: Importing articles

The first step in incremental reading is to import an article to read and learn. Usually you will import an article directly from the Internet.

There are two basic ways of importing an article:

  1. One article - copy the selected text in your web browser to the clipboard, and press Ctrl+Alt+N in SuperMemo
  2. Many articles - open many articles in Internet Explorer, and press Shift+F8 in SuperMemo. You will be able to import selected articles

You can also import articles from Wikipedia with Ctrl+Shift+W

Topic #58: Extracting fragments (2 elements) (top)

During reading, you will extract the most important parts of your article for learning. This is to make sure you do not forget the most important parts of the article.

Extracting a selection will do the following:

  • create a new element/page containing the extracted fragment
  • introduce this new page into the learning process (i.e. repetitions)
  • highlight the extracted fragment in the original article

Topic #45: You can import many articles from Internet Explorer (3 elements) (top)

You can import many articles from Internet Explorer if you press Shift+F8

You can also use Edit : Import web pages (if you select File : Level : Middle)

Topic #63: Converting extracts to questions and answers (9 elements) (top)

You can convert fragments of articles to questions and answers. You will do it using the tool called Cloze. A cloze is a sentence with a word missing and replaced with three dots. Such a sentence can be understood as a question. Your job is to recall the missing part of the sentence. For example:

Sentence: The theory of relativity was proposed by Einstein


Cloze:
     Question: The theory of relativity was proposed by [...]
     Answer: Einstein
