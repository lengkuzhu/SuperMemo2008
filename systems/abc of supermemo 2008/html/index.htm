Topic #3: How does SuperMemo work?

This is how SuperMemo works:
  1. Collect learning material: SuperMemo helps you create collections of facts that you would like to remember
  2. Test your memory: SuperMemo tests your knowledge of individual facts and collects information about your memory
  3. Make smart repetitions: By understanding your memory, SuperMemo finds the optimum timing for review of the learned material
  4. Save time: With minimum repetitions, you waste no time on material you know well

Topic #13: Repetitions (9 elements) (top)

Repetition is the key weapon against forgetting

Everything you learn with SuperMemo will come back to you for review and repetition. Every day, you should review your scheduled portion of the learning material. Start with pressing Ctrl+L, or Enter, or choose Learn (if visible).

Use Enter to go from page to page. Sometimes, SuperMemo will ask you to grade yourself (e.g. Bright, Good, Pass, Fail, etc.). In such cases, click the grade that best reflects how well you remember

Topic #6: Learning with SuperMemo (17 elements) (top)

To use SuperMemo, you only need to learn two keystrokes:

  • Ctrl+L - make repetitions of the learning material (Learn)
  • Alt+A - to add new questions and answers (Add new)

Topic #2: Dismiss learning material (3 elements) (top)

If you see a page that you do not want to learn you can:

  1. Delete it, or
  2. Dismiss it (i.e. remove from the learning process without deleting)

To dismiss a page press Ctrl+D.

This will keep the page in your collection but you will not have it presented for review.

Topic #20: Backup (3 elements) (top)

That's all! You have just learned all the basics of SuperMemo! Now you will need to trust the program for some time before you can see the effects of your work. Your trust will grow if you know how SuperMemo works (see: Principles of SuperMemo at supermemo.com).

Use Learn button daily to make sure you do not forget what you have learned. Use Add new (Alt+A) to add new questions and answers.

Do not forget to back-up your learning material from time to time. Use Ctrl+Shift+C (Copy collection) to copy your material to some location on your hard disk. Or better yet: on a DVD-R or a flash drive.
You have two B-choices: Back up or Be sorry!

To learn more advanced options of SuperMemo, visit 25 Steps to SuperMemo Mastery at supermemo.com.

Topic #34: Incremental reading (26 elements) (top)

Even though you have already learned the basics of SuperMemo, you should know that the program provides you with a yet more powerful learning weapon: Incremental reading.

Incremental reading is more complex than basic SuperMemo, and may require more training. But it will help you read thousands of articles imported from the Internet. It will also help you quickly convert these articles into rock-solid knowlege.

Press Enter for some incremental reading hints.
